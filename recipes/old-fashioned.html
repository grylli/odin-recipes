Old Fashioned

"Old Fashioned cocktail at Kogo" by Bex.Walton is licensed under CC BY 2.0 .

Probably the oldest cocktail that was called a "cocktail", the Old Fashioned is a straightforward, easy-to-make and easy-to-drink cocktail with lots of flexibility. As long as you have a glass and the ingredients, you can make an Old Fashioned!

Old Fashioneds are such a simple formula that they're robust to easy changes. Swap your whiskey for brandy, or maybe use maple syrup instead of sugar. The possibilities are endless!

Ingredients

  • 2 oz American whiskey
  • 1 tsp granulated sugar
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 1 swathe orange peel

Steps

  1. Prepare your garnish. Use a peel to remove a piece of skin from an orange. Take care to remove as little pith - the white stuff - as possible.
  2. Put your sugar into your glass.
  3. Shake 2 dashes of bitters onto the sugar. Stir until sugar is coated by the bitters.
  4. Measure and pour your whiskey on top of the sugar-bitters mixture.
  5. Build a good amount of ice into your glass. Stir for 5-10 seconds.
  6. Squeeze your orange peel over the top of the drink, then twist and place inside.
  7. Serve!
